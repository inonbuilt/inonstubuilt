What is SPOM in CA Final? Complete ICAI New Scheme Guide (2026)

SPOM (Self-Paced Online Modules) is a mandatory assessment system introduced by ICAI under the New Scheme of Education and Training 2023. If you are preparing for CA Final 2026, understanding what is SPOM in CA Final is extremely important for your eligibility and academic planning.

SPOM is conducted as a Computer Based Test (CBT) and consists entirely of objective type MCQs. Students must clear required SPOM sets before appearing for CA Final examination as applicable under ICAI norms.

Why ICAI Introduced SPOM Under New Scheme 2023

ICAI introduced SPOM to reduce final exam burden and promote structured self-learning during Articleship. The objective is to strengthen conceptual clarity, improve professional judgment and align CA qualification with global standards.

Structure of SPOM in CA Final

Set A – Corporate & Economic Laws (Mandatory)

Set A focuses on Companies Act provisions, compliance requirements, timelines, penalties and economic laws such as FEMA and PMLA.

Nature of Questions:

  • Section-based MCQs
  • Timeline-based questions
  • Penalty and compliance scenarios
  • Application-based case studies

Difficulty level is moderate and requires strong conceptual clarity.

Set B – Strategic Cost & Performance Management (Mandatory)

Set B includes costing numericals, decision-making problems and performance evaluation techniques.

Nature of Questions:

  • Numerical MCQs
  • Short computational problems
  • Decision-making case studies
  • Logical analysis questions

Students who practice calculations regularly find this set highly scoring.

Set C – Elective (Choose One Specialization)

Students must choose one SPOM elective based on interest and career goals. Options include Risk Management, Financial Services & Capital Markets, International Taxation, Economic Laws and Global Financial Reporting Standards.

Before selecting elective, consider:

  • Your career interest
  • Articleship exposure
  • Strength in law, finance or taxation
  • Future professional plans

Read detailed guide: SPOM Elective Selection Guide

Set D – Multi-Disciplinary Case Study

Set D consists of integrated case-based MCQs testing real-life professional judgment and analytical ability.

SPOM Passing Criteria

  • Minimum 50% required in each set
  • Computer Based Test (CBT)
  • Objective type MCQs
  • No negative marking (as per current structure)
  • Unlimited attempts allowed

Although attempts are unlimited, delay in clearing SPOM may delay CA Final eligibility.

When Should You Complete SPOM?

The ideal time to clear SPOM ICAI New Scheme sets is during Articleship.

  • Month 1–2 → Set A
  • Month 3–4 → Set B
  • Month 5 → Elective (Set C)
  • Month 6 → Set D

Finishing SPOM early reduces final exam pressure significantly.

Common Mistakes Students Make in SPOM

  • Treating SPOM casually
  • Not practicing numericals
  • Choosing wrong elective
  • Ignoring amendments
  • Postponing SPOM until final attempt

How to Clear SPOM in First Attempt

  • Complete learning modules seriously
  • Make short revision notes
  • Practice MCQs regularly
  • Revise amendments carefully
  • Attempt mock tests

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is SPOM in CA Final?

SPOM stands for Self-Paced Online Modules introduced by ICAI under New Scheme 2023. It is a mandatory CBT exam consisting of MCQs.

Is SPOM compulsory?

Yes, SPOM is mandatory for students registered under the ICAI New Scheme.

What is the passing criteria?

Students must score minimum 50% in each SPOM set.

How many attempts are allowed?

Unlimited attempts are allowed with fresh registration.
